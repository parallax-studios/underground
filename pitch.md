## THE CORE

*[REED speaks]*

### Core Loop

**The 30-second loop** is always about reading, reacting, and expressing. Whether you're threading a drift through traffic, pulling a crossover on the court, landing a trick combo, or throwing hands in a circle — the fundamental verb is the same: **read the situation, choose your style, execute with flair**. Points don't just come from winning. They come from HOW you win.

**The 5-minute loop**: Enter a street event (race, fight, pickup game, trick challenge) → perform with style → earn REP and CASH → spend on gear/car parts/moves → unlock new turf and rivals → repeat at higher stakes.

**The session loop (30-60 min)**: Work a district. Build your name. Attract the district boss's attention. Challenge them. Claim the turf. Each district has a dominant discipline — Southside is racing, the Docks are fighting, Midtown is balling — but you need to be decent at everything to survive anywhere.

### Core Mechanics

1. **STYLE METER (Universal)** — Every action across all disciplines feeds a shared style meter. Drifting, ankle-breaking, combo-linking, trash-talking back — it all fills the same bar. A full style meter triggers "UNDERGROUND MODE" — time slows, your moves hit harder, your car grips better, the crowd loses it.

2. **REP SYSTEM (Progression)** — Reputation is your XP, currency, and access pass. REP is earned through performance but WEIGHTED by difficulty, style, and crowd reaction. The system tracks your playstyle patterns and the world reacts.

3. **BUILD SYSTEM (Customization-as-Mastery)** — Cars, outfits, movesets, trick loadouts with real tradeoffs. A heavy car drifts different from a light one. A flashy fighting style leaves you open. Discovering synergies between gear, moves, and disciplines is the deep layer.

4. **RIVAL AI (The Modern Engine in Disguise)** — Every named rival is a full AI personality. They have moods, grudges, respect thresholds, and playstyle signatures. Beat someone badly and they come back harder — or break. Show mercy and they might join your crew. This is NOT scripted — it's an AI agent system generating emergent rivalries.

5. **DISCIPLINE SWITCHING (Genre Fluidity)** — The player is never locked into one genre. The deepest rewards come from cross-discipline mastery — a signature move that references your racing record in a fight, ball-handling that borrows from trick combos.

### What Mastery Looks Like

**Beginner**: Wins races by staying on the road. Wins fights by mashing. Gets by.

**Intermediate**: Chains style across events. Realizes drift bonuses fund better fighting movesets. Reads rival patterns. Builds around synergies.

**Expert**: The city is a playground. They know which rivals to provoke for max REP. Their car is tuned for their exact racing line. They chain UNDERGROUND MODE across discipline switches. They're playing Football Manager through the lens of a street legend.

### Unique Mechanic: CROSS-DISCIPLINE STYLE CHAINING

No game has ever let you carry momentum across genres in a single session. Drift a perfect lap, step out, carry that style energy into a streetball game, use the overflow to trigger a devastating special in a fight. The style meter is the Rosetta Stone that translates between disciplines.

### Risk Assessment

- **Jack of all trades, master of none**: Each discipline must feel tight enough to stand alone. Mitigation: Each has its own depth tree.
- **Overwhelm at onboarding**: Four genres is a lot. Mitigation: Opening hours focus on ONE discipline (player's choice).
- **REP grinding**: Mitigation: REP growth tied to NOVELTY — repeating the same event type yields diminishing returns.

## THE HEART

*[NOVA speaks]*

### Theme

**UNDERGROUND is about becoming someone when the world has decided you're no one.**

A story about identity forged in competition. About the difference between being KNOWN and being RESPECTED. About the intoxication of fame on a small stage and the cost of climbing higher. When you built yourself from nothing, how much of yourself do you sell when the spotlight finds you?

This is not a rags-to-riches story. It's a rags-to-*relevance* story.

### The World

The city has no proper name. People call it **"The Underground"** — not because it's literally underground, but because nothing that happens here is on any record. No leagues. No licenses. No cameras. A mid-2000s American city caught between decay and gentrification, where the real culture happens after midnight in places the city pretends don't exist — parking garages turned drift circuits, rooftop basketball courts lit by stolen power, warehouse fight rings.

The wound: **the city is being watched.** Developers, promoters, media vultures — they've noticed the underground scene. They want to package it, sell it, clean it up. The deeper you go, the more you feel the tension between keeping it real and cashing in.

### The Player's Emotional Arc

**HOUR 1**: You feel small. The city is huge. Your car is trash, your moves are basic. You're invisible. The game WANTS you to feel the hunger.

**HOUR 10**: You've got a name in one district. An NPC references something you did three sessions ago. The AI remembers.

**HOUR 30**: You're a name. A promoter approaches. A news camera shows up. Your crew starts to fracture — some want to go legit, some want to stay underground. Fame has a cost.

**HOUR 50+**: You can become the King of the Underground — respected, feared, legendary, forever in shadows. Or go mainstream — money, fame, but the underground disowns you. Or try both, which is hardest.

### Story Hook

**"Someone recorded your last fight. It's got 2 million views. Everyone in the Underground is talking. Half want to congratulate you. The other half want to bury you before the cameras find them too."**

## THE MARKET

*[HYPE speaks]*

### Target Audience

**Primary**: 25-38 year olds who grew up on PS2/Xbox-era street culture games. They've typed "NFS Underground 2 remaster when?" at least once.

**Secondary**: 18-25 year olds who discovered 2000s culture through TikTok and retro gaming channels.

**Tertiary**: Systems-loving players (Balatro, Factorio crowd) who'll discover the build optimization depth underneath.

### Comparable Games

1. **"If Need for Speed Underground met Yakuza"** — Racing and customization embedded in a living open-world with rivalries and narrative weight.
2. **"If Def Jam: Fight for NY met Football Manager"** — Raw fighting energy with deep AI-driven relationship management.
3. **"If FIFA Street met Balatro"** — Pickup game energy with a build/synergy metagame.
4. **"If Midnight Club met Persona's social sim"** — Street racing where relationships matter as much as driving skill.
5. **"If Tony Hawk's Underground met Nemesis System"** — Trick mastery with AI rivals who remember everything.

### Unique Selling Proposition

**No game has ever unified street racing, street fighting, street sports, and street culture into a single reputation-driven career.** The USP is: **one identity, expressed four ways, in a city that remembers everything you do.**

### Platform Fit

**PC (Steam)**: Primary. The nostalgia gaming audience lives on Steam.
**Console (PS5/Xbox)**: Natural second platform. The original games were console experiences.

## THE SCOPE

### Feature Pillars (Non-Negotiable)

1. **THE FOUR DISCIPLINES** — Racing, Fighting, Street Sports, Freestyle Tricks. Each with its own skill tree, mastery curve, build system. They share the Style Meter.

2. **THE LIVING CITY** — Dense open-world divided into districts. Not GTA's sprawl — Yakuza's density. Day/night cycle with different events.

3. **THE REP ENGINE** — AI-driven reputation tracking everything. Your playstyle, win/loss, rivals, crew, fashion, mercy or ruthlessness.

4. **THE RIVAL SYSTEM** — AI agent personalities with moods, wounds, grudges, respect. They evolve, remember, surprise.

5. **THE 2000s SKIN** — CRT filter. Chunky PS2-era UI. Hip-hop soundtrack. Flip phone menus. The aesthetic IS the identity.

### Stretch Goals

1. **CREW VS. CREW MULTIPLAYER** — Online asynchronous rivalry between player-built crews.
2. **MUSIC INTEGRATION** — Rhythm-game elements syncing style meter to the beat.
3. **CREATE-A-LEGEND MODE** — Full character creator. Export your legend as an AI rival for others.
4. **DISTRICT EDITOR** — Let players design and share custom districts.
5. **CROSS-PLATFORM RIVAL SHARING** — Your AI-generated rival appears in other players' games.

### Cut List

- Full online real-time multiplayer
- Multiple cities
- Licensed real-world cars
- Skateboarding as a fifth discipline
- Photo-realistic graphics
